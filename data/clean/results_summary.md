## 1. Sample

- **Cleaned ENAHO 2024 microdata**: 113,755 individuals with valid age, after
  dropping 3,966 individuals with missing or fractional age (3.4% of raw).
- **Treatment definition**: `treat = 1` if EDAD ≥ 65, else 0.
  Treated N = 14,088; Control N = 99,667 (full sample, asymmetric).
- **Bandwidth-restricted estimation sample** (h\* = 14.24 yrs): N = 27,809
  (17,047 below cutoff, 10,762 above).
- **Survey weights** (`FACPOB07`) are present in the cleaned file but are
  **not** applied inside `rdrobust`; estimates are sample, not

## 6. Headline conclusion

Crossing the age-65 Pensión 65 institutional threshold is **not associated**
with a statistically detectable jump in either digital wallet ownership or
active use in the bandwidth-restricted ENAHO 2024 sample. The
intent-to-treat estimates are precise enough to rule out effects larger
than ±3.4 percentage points on ownership at conventional levels, and the
randomization p-value of 0.434 confirms the null under permutation
inference. Suggestive heterogeneity by SISFOH points toward a small
positive point estimate concentrated among the extreme-poor target group,
but the subgroup CIs do not exclude zero. We interpret the findings as
consistent with institutional digitization being insufficient on its own
to overcome the secular age gradient in digital adoption, and motivate
complementary supply-side policies (smartphone access, digital literacy
training).
